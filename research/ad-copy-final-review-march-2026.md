# Final Ad Copy Coherence & Compliance Review
*Research memo — March 19, 2026*
*Question: Do the 3 final locked ad concepts pass a top-to-bottom coherence test and comply with Instagram/Meta ad specifications?*

## TL;DR

Two compliance issues found that need fixing before launch. (1) All 3 headlines exceed Instagram's 40-character recommended limit and will likely truncate on mobile — all need shortening. (2) Concept 1's primary text first line is 126 characters — 1 character over the 125-char truncation gate, cutting off the period after "once." Easy fixes. Copy coherence is strong across all 3 concepts after the changes made in this session. One minor copy quality flag on Concept 1 (the word "dimensions" appears without context) and one note on Concept 4's "analyzed" consistency.

## Compliance Check

### Primary Text — 125-Character Truncation Gate

| Concept | First Line | Chars | Status |
|---------|-----------|-------|--------|
| 1 | "We're going to pass for now, but please keep us updated." You've gotten this email from an investor. Probably more than once. | 126 | OVER BY 1 — period after "once" gets cut |
| 2 | You're in a pitch meeting. The investor says "This is really interesting." You're thinking: great sign. You're wrong. | 117 | PASSES |
| 4 | After 2,500+ founder pitches analyzed, the pattern is always the same: The best founders don't have better ideas. | 113 | PASSES |

**Concept 1 fix:** Drop "Probably more than once." from the first visible line. Move it to the second paragraph. Revised first 125:

`"We're going to pass for now, but please keep us updated." You've gotten this email from an investor.` — 99 chars. Comfortably within limit. "Probably more than once." becomes the first line of the expanded text (after the user taps "...more").

### Headlines — 40-Character Recommended Limit

Instagram headlines display approximately 27-40 characters on mobile before truncation. The recommended safe limit is 40 characters (Meta Business Help Center, Ignite Social Media 2025 spec guide, SaveMyLeads).

| Concept | Headline | Chars | Status |
|---------|----------|-------|--------|
| 1 | What Investors See When You Pitch — But Won't Tell You | 54 | OVER — truncates mid-sentence |
| 2 | Most Founders Misread Investor Signals. Do You? | 47 | OVER — "Do You?" may get cut |
| 4 | See What Investors See — But They'll Never Tell You | 51 | OVER — truncates after the dash |

**Important context:** Headlines in Instagram feed ads appear on the CTA strip below the image — but Instagram doesn't always show them. Facebook placements do show them more consistently. Even when shown, truncation at ~40 characters means the user only sees:

- C1: "What Investors See When You Pitch —..." (the payoff "But Won't Tell You" gets cut)
- C2: "Most Founders Misread Investor Signal..." (truncates mid-word)
- C4: "See What Investors See — But They'l..." (truncates mid-word)

**Recommended fixes:**

| Concept | Current (too long) | Proposed (under 40) | Chars |
|---------|-------------------|---------------------|-------|
| 1 | What Investors See When You Pitch — But Won't Tell You | What Investors See (But Won't Say) | 34 |
| 2 | Most Founders Misread Investor Signals. Do You? | Do You Misread Investor Signals? | 32 |
| 4 | See What Investors See — But They'll Never Tell You | See What Investors Really See | 28 |

These shorter versions preserve the core message and fit within the 40-char limit. Alternative options if Brian prefers different phrasing:
- C1 alt: "What Investors Won't Tell You" (29 chars)
- C2 alt: "Are You Misreading Investors?" (29 chars)
- C4 alt: "What 2,500 Pitches Revealed" (27 chars)

## Coherence Review — Concept by Concept

### Concept 1: "The Polite Pass"

**Image** (guy in hoodie, hand on forehead, laptop in coffee shop) **→ Overlay** ("We're going to pass." — every investor): Strong. The image shows rejection; the overlay verbalizes it. Instant story.

**Overlay → Primary text:** Strong. The overlay is the short version; the primary text expands to the full email quote. Natural continuation.

**Primary text internal flow:** Strong through "patterns they see in the first 5 minutes but will never point out." Then: "This free 3-minute assessment shows you what investors actually see when you pitch." — clean transition to the product.

**One flag:** "4 scores across the dimensions that matter." A cold reader doesn't know what "dimensions" means here. Unlike "4 Scores. 2 Minutes." in the old Concept 4 headline (which we killed for this exact reason), this is buried in the body text rather than a headline, so it's less jarring — the user has already been hooked by this point. But "dimensions" is still jargon. Consider: "4 scores across what actually matters" or "Scored on the 4 things that actually matter." This is a minor flag — not a dealbreaker but worth considering.

**Primary text → Headline → CTA:** Coherent flow from promise to action.

**Identity signaling check:** "Investor" appears in overlay, primary text (3x), and headline. "Pitch" appears in primary text and headline. A founder scrolling Instagram will recognize this is for them within the first second.

### Concept 2: "The Room You Can't Read"

**Image** (founder facing VC in Patagonia vest, arms crossed) **→ Overlay** ("He thinks the pitch is going well. / The investor tuned out five minutes ago."): Strong. The image shows the disconnect; the overlay narrates it. The irony between the founder's earnest expression and the VC's closed body language is exactly what the overlay describes.

**Overlay → Primary text:** Smooth after the tense fix. Overlay is present tense ("thinks," "tuned out"). Primary text is now present tense ("You're in a pitch meeting"). Same temporal frame. The scene shifts from third person (overlay: "He thinks") to second person (primary: "You're in a pitch meeting") — this is intentional and effective. The overlay observes someone else; the primary text puts you in the room. It's a perspective flip that makes the reader think "wait, is this me?"

**Primary text internal flow:** Tight. "You're thinking: great sign. You're wrong." — punchy. The explanation of what "interesting" actually means is specific and insightful. "It's a test most founders don't know they're taking." — great closing line for the hook section.

**Primary text → Headline → CTA:** "The signals investors send are almost never what they seem" flows into "Do You Misread Investor Signals?" (proposed shorter headline) — direct question to the reader. Strong.

**Identity signaling check:** "Pitch" in overlay, "investor" in overlay + primary text (4x), "founders" in primary text. Strong signaling.

### Concept 4: "Built From the Other Side"

**Image** (mentor portrait, bookshelves) **→ Overlay** ("2,500 founder pitches analyzed. / See what investors see."): Strong. Authority portrait + specific number = instant credibility. "See what investors see" is the hook.

**Overlay → Primary text:** "After 2,500+ founder pitches analyzed, the pattern is always the same:" — natural continuation of the overlay's claim. The colon creates a curiosity gap.

**One consistency note:** The overlay says "2,500 founder pitches analyzed" (no plus sign). The primary text says "2,500+ founder pitches analyzed" (with plus sign). Minor but should match. Recommend adding the "+" to the overlay: "2,500+ founder pitches analyzed."

**Primary text internal flow:** Strong. "The best founders don't have better ideas. They understand how investors think." — core HC insight in two sentences. The specific examples that follow demonstrate authority through pattern recognition rather than claiming it. "This assessment was built by someone who's raised capital and invested it" — connects the portrait to the assessment.

**Primary text → Headline → CTA:** "to show founders the gaps nobody in their world will point out" flows into "See What Investors Really See" (proposed shorter headline). The headline echoes the overlay's "See what investors see." Clean.

**Identity signaling check:** "Founder" in overlay + primary text (2x), "investors" in overlay + primary text (3x), "pitch" in overlay + primary text. Strongest identity signaling of the three concepts.

## Recommendation

### Must fix before launch:

1. **Concept 1 primary text:** Move "Probably more than once." after the truncation break. First visible line becomes: `"We're going to pass for now, but please keep us updated." You've gotten this email from an investor.` (99 chars)

2. **All 3 headlines:** Shorten to under 40 characters. Proposed:
   - C1: "What Investors See (But Won't Say)" (34 chars)
   - C2: "Do You Misread Investor Signals?" (32 chars)
   - C4: "See What Investors Really See" (28 chars)

3. **Concept 4 overlay:** Add "+" to match primary text: "2,500+ founder pitches analyzed."

4. **Landing page social proof:** Update "pitches analyzed" → "pitches analyzed" to match all ad copy.

### Consider but not blocking:

5. **Concept 1:** "4 scores across the dimensions that matter" — "dimensions" is mild jargon. Consider "4 scores across what actually matters" for clarity. Not a dealbreaker.

## Pressure Test

*Consequence check: moderate — these are the actual ads that will spend real money. The compliance issues (truncation, headline length) are factual constraints that will definitely cause truncation on mobile. The headline truncation is the more consequential issue: a headline that cuts mid-word looks unprofessional and wastes the CTA strip real estate. The primary text 1-char overshoot on Concept 1 loses a period — minor but sloppy. All fixes are simple text edits.*

No red team needed — these are factual compliance issues, not strategic judgment calls.
