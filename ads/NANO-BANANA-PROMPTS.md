# Nano Banana — HC Ad Reference Images

## Context (paste this first, before any image prompts)

I'm creating reference images for Instagram feed ads (1:1 or 4:5) for a product called Humble Conviction. HC is a free 3-minute online assessment that scores startup founders on 4 dimensions investors actually care about: Clarity, Investor Fluency, Self-Awareness, and Persuasion Instincts. It's built from 2,500+ pitches coached by someone who's been on both sides of the table — raising capital and investing it.

The target audience is male tech founders, 24-34, who are actively fundraising or about to start. They don't know what they don't know — they're getting polite passes from investors and can't figure out why. Think Dunning-Kruger applied to pitching.

I need 4 reference images, each showing a different emotional angle. These are NOT final ads — they're visual direction for an ad creative tool. Each image should feel candid, editorial, documentary-style — like a journalist caught a real moment. The absolute enemy is stock photo energy.

The people in the images should look like the target audience: white males, late 20s to early 30s, good-looking, floppy or wavy hair, wearing hoodies, crewnecks, quarter-zips. Real startup guys, not models.

I'll prompt you one image at a time. Let's establish the visual style on Image 1, then I'll feed you the rest sequentially.

---

## Image 1: "The Polite Pass"

**What this image needs to make the viewer feel:** The gut-punch of rejection — that private moment when you open the email and realize you didn't get the deal. The viewer scrolling Instagram should feel a pang of recognition. "I've been that guy."

**Literal description:**
Young white male founder, late 20s, messy brown hair, wearing a dark hoodie over a t-shirt. Sitting alone at a coffee shop table with his laptop open in front of him. He just read something devastating on the screen — his face shows that sinking "oh shit" feeling, jaw tight, eyes slightly wide, one hand on his forehead. Not crying, not dramatic — just that private moment where the wind goes out of you. Coffee shop is a real indie spot — exposed brick, warm overhead lighting, other people blurred in the background going about their day while his just fell apart. Natural light mixed with warm interior lighting. Candid, unstaged feel — like someone across the room caught this moment on their phone.

---

## Image 2: "The Room You Can't Read"

**What this image needs to make the viewer feel:** The uncomfortable realization that you might be the last person in the room to know it's not going well. Confidence meeting reality. The viewer should think: "Wait... have I been that guy and not known it?"

**Literal description:**
Small round conference table, tight framing. A young white male founder, early 30s, clean-cut with slightly floppy hair, wearing a navy quarter-zip pullover. He's standing/leaning over the table, mid-pitch, grinning, gesturing with both hands open — he clearly thinks he's crushing it. Two investors sit on either side of the round table: a woman in a blazer looking down at her phone, a man with silver hair resting his chin in his hand, eyes half-closed with boredom. Coffee mugs on the table. The room is a standard corporate conference room — not glamorous. The disconnect between his energy and theirs is the whole story. Shot tight so all three faces are readable. Natural office lighting, no filters.

---

## Image 3: "The Shift"

**What this image needs to make the viewer feel:** Aspiration and intrigue. The founder who figured it out — he's not trying harder, he's doing something fundamentally different. The viewer should think: "What does that guy know that I don't?"

**Literal description:**
Casual coffee meeting in a bright upscale café or hotel lobby. A young white male founder, late 20s, good-looking, wavy hair, wearing a fitted navy crewneck sweater. He's sitting across a small marble table from a VC investor — late 30s, clean-cut, wearing a Patagonia-style fleece vest over a button-down. The founder is leaning back, one arm draped over the back of his chair, slight knowing smile — completely unhurried. The investor is leaning forward across the table, gesturing with both hands, clearly animated and engaged. The power dynamic has flipped — the founder is the one being pursued. Marble table, two coffees, phone face-down, natural daylight from big windows, plants in background. Shot at table level, intimate framing. Warm, editorial feel.

## Image 4: "Built From the Other Side"

**What this image needs to make the viewer feel:** Trust and credibility. This is the person behind the assessment — someone who's seen thousands of pitches and knows exactly where you're going wrong. The viewer should think: "That guy could probably tell me something I need to hear."

**Literal description:**
Portrait of an older male mentor figure, late 40s to early 50s, silver at the temples, wearing a quality crewneck sweater. Sitting in a home office or study — real bookshelves behind him, natural light from a window to one side. His expression is warm but direct — slight knowing smile, the look of someone who's heard a thousand pitches and knows exactly where you're going wrong. Reading glasses pushed up on his forehead. Head and shoulders framing, eye-level. The vibe is "the guy you'd want in your corner" — experienced, no-bullshit, approachable. Natural light, no retouching.
